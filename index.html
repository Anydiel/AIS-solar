🏠 STEP 1: Roof Railing Installation
🔧 Locate rafters → Install L-feet with lag bolts (8mm x 60mm) → Attach aluminum rails → Use level → Seal penetrations with roofing sealant.
☀️ STEP 2: MC4 Connectors & Panel Wiring
🔴 SERIES: Panel1 NEG (-) → Panel2 POS (+). Voltage adds, current stays same.
🟢 PARALLEL: All POS together, all NEG together. Current adds, voltage stays same.
⚡ STEP 3: MPPT Connection Order (CRITICAL)
🔴 CRITICAL SEQUENCE: 1) Connect Battery to MPPT FIRST → 2) Connect Solar Panels to MPPT → 3) Never reverse this order!
🔋 STEP 4: Battery Wiring
🔴 SERIES (24V from two 12V): BAT A NEG (-) → BAT B POS (+). System POS → BAT A POS. System NEG → BAT B NEG.
🔴 PARALLEL (more capacity): All POS together, all NEG together.
🔌 STEP 5: Inverter Connection
🔴 Battery POSITIVE (+) → T-Class Fuse (within 7 inches of battery) → Inverter DC+.
🔴 Battery NEGATIVE (-) → Inverter DC- directly.
⛓️ STEP 6: Earthing (Grounding) - SAFETY CRITICAL
⛓️ Connect ALL metal parts (panel frames, rails, MPPT case, inverter case) to earth ground rod (copper rod, 8ft depth). Use 8 AWG green/yellow wire.

📖 Solar Terminology Glossary ▼

☀️ PANEL SPECIFICATIONS
VocVoltage Open Circuit — Voltage when panels are disconnected. Used for max string voltage calculation.
VmpVoltage at Maximum Power — Operating voltage when panel produces peak power.
ImpCurrent at Maximum Power — Operating current when panel produces peak power.
IscShort Circuit Current — Max current when panel terminals are shorted. Used for fuse/breaker sizing.
⚡ CHARGE CONTROLLER
MPPTMaximum Power Point Tracking — Smart controller that adjusts voltage/current to get max power from panels.
PWMPulse Width Modulation — Simpler controller. Less efficient than MPPT.
🔋 BATTERY & BMS
SoCState of Charge — Battery percentage (100% = full).
DoDDepth of Discharge — How much battery is used. 80% DoD means 20% remaining.
LVDLow Voltage Disconnect — Voltage at which inverter shuts off to protect battery.
LVRLow Voltage Reconnect — Voltage at which inverter turns back on after recharging.
HVDHigh Voltage Disconnect — Voltage at which charge controller stops charging.
BMSBattery Management System — Protects lithium batteries (voltage, temperature, balancing).
CAN BusController Area Network — Fast communication protocol between battery and inverter.
RS485Serial communication standard. Common in Pylontech, Dyness batteries.
C-RateCharge/discharge rate relative to battery capacity. 0.5C = half of battery Ah per hour.
📏 UNITS & MEASUREMENTS
AhAmp-Hour — Battery capacity unit. Multiply by voltage to get Watt-hours.
Wh / kWhWatt-Hour / Kilowatt-Hour — Energy unit. 1kWh = 1,000 watt-hours.
AWGAmerican Wire Gauge — Wire thickness. Smaller number = thicker wire.
🔌 WIRING CONFIGURATIONS
Series (S)Wiring that adds voltage, current stays same. Example: 4S = voltage ×4.
Parallel (P)Wiring that adds current, voltage stays same. Example: 2P = current ×2.
StringA group of panels wired in series. Multiple strings can be connected in parallel.
🏠 SYSTEM TYPES
Grid-TieSystem feeds power to utility grid. Shuts down when grid fails. No battery backup.
Off-GridIndependent system with battery. No grid connection.
HybridWorks with grid AND battery. Can charge from grid or solar, can sell excess.
🧪 BATTERY CHEMISTRY
LiFePO4Lithium Iron Phosphate — Safest lithium battery. 80% usable. Recommended for solar.
AGMAbsorbent Glass Mat — Sealed lead-acid battery. 50% usable capacity.
GelGel lead-acid battery. 50% usable capacity.
🛡️ SAFETY & PROTECTION
T-Class FuseHigh-rupture capacity fuse required for lithium batteries.
Grounding / EarthingConnect all metal parts to earth ground rod for safety.
